Team Profile



Marek Ivanov

Marek Ivanov was born in Estonia in the year 1995 AD. After completeing year 12, he studied diligently to be an electrician and once graduating, quickly started working manufacturing precast concrete elements for buildings in across Finland. Marek came to Australia in the year 2013 AD. He started working in a custom concrete furniture studio while at the same time started learning about CNC machines, 3D printers and lasers and 3D modelling. 5 years later got a job in a modelmaking studio in Perth that uses top of the range 3d printers to make dinosaurs for museums and shows. He has been working with all aspects of 3D printing, prototyping, mold making, casting with resins and other composite materials as a freelancer and has been apart of many such projects. Marek is fluent in Estonian and English but also can speak Russian and bit of German. Marek remembers his first computer lesson fondly as a "mind blowing" experience, "The things that those computers could do was amazing". Like every other young student, Marek was in it for the games. He Played Doom3D a lot and over the years all manner of games. Seeing the rapid development in that area, from floppy discs to portable mp3 players, he was truly in awe technology, and the start of his initial interest of IT. 20 years on, Marek has learned to use many different tools from media editing, CAD design, Z Brush sculpting for 3D printing and rapid prototyping. Last Christmas, He was working on a shopfitting project where his company had to make some display windows move with stepper motors and Arduino boards. Initially he thought that would be easy to do and without much prior background took the lead on the task, quickly he understood that he didn't understand anything about it. Luckily, a friend of a friend saved the day, or night, as it were, writing the programs throughout the night in the shop. The reason Marek chose RMIT is that it gives the flexibility with time management, but also there is a sense of urgency. Having the opportunity to study on the road is great, as he likes to travel around quite a bit. So far lack of urgency has really stopped him from progressing forward with his skills. He Also likes that everything is well structured to support the learning process. Marek expect to learn new skills auch as being proficient in programming languages and techiques, and network systems. He is Looking forward to understand the methods and tools used in the computer world. Hopefully able to write useful programs that make life better at work and home.
